How-to Walkthrough · Microsoft Scout

Have Your Microsoft Scout Build Your Second Brain

A copy-paste walkthrough — your AI colleague does the setup while you watch. Three sections, ~30–45 minutes, 11 prompts.

The idea A Second Brain is an LLM-maintained personal wiki (Karpathy's pattern). You provide the sources and the questions; Microsoft Scout is the librarian — it summarizes, cross-references, files, and keeps the books. Paste the 11 prompts below, approve each step, and you'll end up with the same working system, entirely in plain markdown you own.

What we'll cover: 1 · What is Microsoft Scout 2 · What is a Second Brain 3 · The setup — 11 prompts

Section 01 · The Platform

What is Microsoft Scout?

A desktop AI assistant with hands-on access to your tools and data — it doesn't just chat, it acts.

🧩

Skills

Why use it — four reasons it stops feeling like a chatbot

Works the way real colleagues do

Reads your inbox, preps your meetings, files your notes, pushes back when you're wrong, remembers what you said last week.

Composes with the M365 stack

"Summarize today's meetings into my project notebook" — pulled, drafted, filed in one turn.

Customizable to who you are

Personality, voice, operating rules, skills, memory — all configurable. Fits your workflow, not the other way around.

Autonomous when you want it

Schedule a nightly knowledge consolidation. Trigger a daily inbox triage. It works while you don't have to.

Section 02 · The Concept

What is a Second Brain?

Five reasons it matters — what changes when your knowledge stops leaking

Your knowledge stops leaking

Conversations, articles, decisions become durable, linked pages your assistant can recall instantly.

Context becomes free

Every new question starts with the answer to the last hundred. The assistant gets more useful the more you use it.

Cross-reference is automatic

When a source contradicts something you wrote three months ago, the assistant flags it — relationships you'd never spot.

You own it

Plain markdown files. Opens in Obsidian, Notepad, anything. No vendor lock-in. Ever.

Pairs with your assistant's identity

SOUL.md (voice) + Second Brain (knowledge) = a real coworker who knows you.

Three principles — an LLM-maintained wiki, three layers, clear ownership

1 · Three layers, clear ownership

Raw sources are immutable inputs you provide. The wiki is owned by your AI. The schema is co-evolved between you.

2 · The assistant is the librarian

You provide sources and ask questions. The assistant summarizes, cross-references, files, and bookkeeps. Not your job.

3 · Compounding knowledge

Every ingest touches 5–15 pages. Every good question becomes a new page. The wiki gets denser, not bigger.

Step 0

Brief your Microsoft Scout

Set the scene before the first command.

▶ Paste this into Microsoft Scout:

I'm setting up a Second Brain — an LLM-maintained personal wiki
using the Karpathy pattern. I want you to build and configure it
for me.

I'll paste a series of setup prompts. For each one:
  1. Confirm what you're about to do.
  2. Do it.
  3. Report back briefly and wait for the next prompt.

My vault location: <vault path, e.g. C:\Users\you\OneDrive\Documents\Scout\SecondBrain>
My Scout's name: <pick a name>
Ready?
Step 1

Build the vault structure

Folders + empty placeholder files.

▶ Paste this into Microsoft Scout:

Create my Second Brain vault at <vault path> with this structure:

  AGENTS.md, README.md, index.md, log.md
  raw/      00-Inbox/      50-Archive/
  10-Notes/entities/   10-Notes/concepts/
  20-Projects/   30-Areas/   40-Resources/
  _meta/conventions.md   _meta/MOCs/   _meta/templates/

Make the folders and empty placeholder files now. We'll fill
AGENTS.md and conventions.md in the next two prompts.
Step 2

Write AGENTS.md (the schema)

The most important file in the vault — read first on every session.

▶ Paste this into Microsoft Scout:

Write AGENTS.md at the vault root following Karpathy's pattern:

  1. Three layers (raw / wiki / schema) and ownership.
  2. Folder conventions and naming rules.
  3. Page types and required YAML frontmatter:
       title, type, created, updated, tags, sources, status
  4. Operations:
       - ingest: raw → summary + entities/concepts + index + log
       - query:  read index → drill → synthesize → file good answers
       - lint:   contradictions / orphans / stale / missing
  5. Log format (greppable, dated entries).
  6. House rules: one idea/page, cite sources, flag uncertainty.

Show me a draft. Take my edits. Then save.
Step 3

Write _meta/conventions.md

The style guide — short, prescriptive, durable.

▶ Paste this into Microsoft Scout:

Write _meta/conventions.md covering:

- File naming (kebab-case for atomic notes,
  YYYY-MM-DD-slug for inbox captures)
- YAML frontmatter template with examples
- Link conventions ([[wiki-links]])
- Tag conventions
- MOC location and naming
- Weekly Inbox processing checklist

Keep it short and prescriptive. Save when I approve.
Step 4

Seed three real pages

An empty wiki is intimidating. Give your Scout examples to follow.

▶ Paste this into Microsoft Scout:

Seed three pages with proper frontmatter and starter content:

  1. index.md (vault root) — empty catalog with sections for
     entities, concepts, summaries, MOCs.
  2. _meta/MOCs/home.md — my landing-page Map of Content,
     curated links to the most important entry points.
  3. 10-Notes/entities/<pick-something-I-care-about>.md —
     a real entity. Ask me what to use before writing.

Show me each one before saving.
Step 5

First ingest — the magic moment

Where the Second Brain becomes real.

▶ Paste this into Microsoft Scout:

I'm going to give you a source: <URL or file path>.

Follow the AGENTS.md ingest operation exactly:
  1. Read the source fully. Don't skim.
  2. Drop a copy or reference into raw/ (immutable).
  3. Summarize 1–3 key takeaways and discuss with me first.
  4. Write a summary page in 40-Resources/ with citations.
  5. Update or create entity pages for any people/orgs/products.
  6. Update or create concept pages for any ideas worth tracking.
  7. Update relevant MOCs in _meta/MOCs/.
  8. Update index.md. Append a dated entry to log.md.

Tell me each page you touched so I can see the cascade.
ExpectedA single ingest typically touches 5–15 pages. That cascade is the wiki getting denser.
Step 6

First query

Test that the wiki is now answering for itself.

▶ Paste this into Microsoft Scout:

Using only the wiki — no fresh web search — answer this:
  <a question that overlaps the source you just ingested>

Follow the AGENTS.md query operation:
  1. Read index.md first.
  2. Drill into the relevant pages, follow [[links]].
  3. Synthesize a clear answer with citations to wiki pages.
  4. If non-trivial, offer to file the answer back as a new
     analysis page so the next query starts smarter.
Step 7

First lint pass

After 5–10 sources, run the maintenance pass.

▶ Paste this into Microsoft Scout:

Run the lint operation from AGENTS.md. Look for:

  - Contradictions across pages
  - Orphans (pages nothing links to)
  - Stale claims (older than 90 days, marked evergreen)
  - Implicit concepts (mentioned often, no page yet)
  - Missing cross-references
  - Inbox backlog

Produce a short report. Wait for my approval before fixing.
Step 8 · optional

Add structured claims

Machine-readable assertions with confidence and evidence.

▶ Paste this into Microsoft Scout:

Extend AGENTS.md with an optional claims block in frontmatter:

  claims:
    - id: claim-001
      text: "<assertion>"
      confidence: 0.0–1.0
      status: provisional | evergreen | disputed
      evidence:
        - source: raw/<file>
          kind: documentation | quote | observation
          excerpt: "<short quote>"
      captured: YYYY-MM-DD
      updated: YYYY-MM-DD

Update lint to flag: disputed claims, no-evidence claims,
contradictions across pages, stale evergreen claims.

▶ Paste this into Microsoft Scout:

Ask me about your name, role, voice, communication style, drives, and
operating rules. Write SOUL.md and AGENTS.md to ~/.copilot/<name>/.
Create MEMORY.md and DREAMS.md (empty seeds) at SecondBrain/_meta/<name>/.
Create a slash-skill /<name> that loads SOUL.md + AGENTS.md at session start.
Save a memory pointer to the file locations. Show drafts before saving.
Step 10 · optional

Schedule the nightly dream loop

Mirrors the light/deep/REM dreaming pattern — runs while you sleep.

▶ Paste this into Microsoft Scout:

Create a Scout automation called "<your-name> Dream Loop"
that runs every day at 11pm. Follow light/deep/REM:

  - Gather candidate facts from the last 24 hours of sessions.
  - Score: frequency (≤0.40) + recency (≤0.20)
         + novelty (≤0.20) + user-confirmation (≤0.20).
  - Promote anything ≥ 0.70 AND (≥2 sessions OR confirmed)
    to MEMORY.md and your permanent memory store.
  - Write a short reflective diary entry to DREAMS.md.
  - Send me a Teams summary when complete.

PRIVACY RULE (non-negotiable):
  Never write raw email, calendar, or chat content to MEMORY.md
  or DREAMS.md. Summarize at whiteboard level. If in doubt, omit.

Create disabled. Dry-run first. Enable only after I approve.
Privacy firstThe dream loop summarizes at whiteboard level only — never raw email, calendar, or chat content. Create it disabled, dry-run it, and enable only after you've reviewed the output.
